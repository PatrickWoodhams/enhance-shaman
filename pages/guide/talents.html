Talents and Build Choices

Enhancement in TBC has one core tree and two common sub spec variants. This page explains why those variants exist and gives you a talent calculator with shareable builds.

Build planning

The two Enhancement variants

In TBC, Enhancement generally locks in a 44 point Enhancement core for the spec defining throughput and raid buffs. The remaining points flex into Elemental or Restoration. These are not different roles. They are two ways to solve different constraints: raising the value of shocks and fire totems, or improving hit, mana efficiency, and totem coverage.

17/44/00 Sub Elemental

Shock tempo Fire totem value Mana smoothing

This variant spends 17 points in Elemental to make shocks and fire totems a larger share of your damage. It is built for players who can consistently spend global cooldowns on shocks without collapsing their mana.

What it gives you

Key talents in this preset: Convection, Concussion, Call of Flame, Reverberation, Elemental Focus, Improved Fire Totems.

  • More shock output through additional shock damage and faster shock cadence.
  • More fire totem damage that supports Fire Nova decisions during fire weaving.
  • Better mana flow so the extra shocks are easier to afford.
When to pick it
  • You already have enough hit from gear and raid buffs.
  • Encounters reward active shock usage and fire totem management.
  • You are optimizing personal damage and can manage mana pressure.
Tradeoffs
  • No Restoration hit talent, so you rely more on gear and raid buffs to stay capped.
  • Totems cost more mana and have less coverage compared to the Restoration variant.
  • Higher shock usage can raise mana pressure if you overcast.
In the calculator, choose the preset 17/44/00 Sub Elemental to load this build, then tailor it for your raid.

03/44/14 Sub Restoration

Hit and consistency Totem mana Totem range

This variant spends 14 points in Restoration to pick up hit and totem efficiency tools that make your damage more stable and your group support easier. It is the practical choice when you value consistency, longer fights, or heavy totem work.

What it gives you

Key talents in this preset: Nature's Guidance, Totemic Focus, Totemic Mastery, plus Concussion on the Elemental side.

  • Hit that reduces misses and frees up gear choices.
  • Lower totem costs which matters in long fights and frequent twisting.
  • Wider totem coverage that helps your party keep buffs without perfect positioning.
  • Better emergency healing as a side benefit of the pathing points.
When to pick it
  • You are early in gearing, hit constrained, or playing without ideal raid buffs.
  • You are twisting often and want the most efficient totem workflow.
  • You want a stable, low friction build that still performs well.
Tradeoffs
  • Fewer Elemental throughput talents, so shocks and fire totems contribute slightly less damage.
  • Lower personal damage ceiling, but higher consistency and a smoother totem workflow.
In the calculator, choose the preset 03/44/14 Sub Restoration to load this build, then tweak based on your group.

Both variants share the same Enhancement core. Your choice is mainly about what you need the extra points to solve: shock and fire throughput, or hit, mana, and totem quality of life.
